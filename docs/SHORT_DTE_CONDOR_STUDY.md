# 1-3DTE condor study — SPY and QQQ (can we mirror these safely?)

**Question (user, 2026-07-09):** the live 1-3DTE record is 9 trades, 5W/0L,
+$94 avg — great but tiny. Before mirroring with real money, validate the cycle
over 5 years, and don't let daily-close marking hide short-DTE gamma.

**Method:** `backtests/short_dte_condor_study.py` — every regime-gated condor
day (same CHOPPY gate as live, 385 days), 0.20-delta shorts solved FOR the short
expiry, $5 wings, 2-trading-day expiry, 70% target, frictions included. Three
levels of honesty:
- optimistic — marked at closes (like older backtests)
- pessimistic — an intraday HIGH/LOW touch of a short strike FORCES exit at that
  day's close ("watchdog fired, user closed")
- pessimistic + 10% entry-credit haircut (the SKEW_STRESS discipline)

## Verdict

1. **SPY 1-3DTE condor: the most robust result in the project.** It survives
   BOTH the touch-forced-exit path model AND the 10% credit haircut with no
   negative years — the same haircut that killed the 45DTE condor backtest
   (-$7.9k). Short cycles capture theta fast enough that the edge doesn't
   depend on model-perfect credits. Cleared for careful live mirroring (1-lot,
   capital ~$380/trade, back in 1-2 days).
2. **QQQ 1-3DTE: positive in every variant but second-tier** (weaker under
   stress, one negative year). Not generated by the system today (the intraday
   scanner is SPY-only); noted as possible future paper-candidate work.
3. Cross-check: the live record (+$94 avg) runs ABOVE the pessimistic model
   ($50) — consistent with real fills being richer than model marks.
4. Unchanged risks: a gap day still takes ~max loss (~$400/lot); losses cluster
   on regime breaks (2019-2020 not in this window). Defined risk + 1-lot sizing
   + the watchdog/gap-check stack remain the protection.
